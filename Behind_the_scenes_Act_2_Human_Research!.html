TRIM33 gene expression & autism symptoms Back to top

Alteration of TRIM33 expression at transcriptional and translational levels is correlated with autism symptoms; 2021, Journal of Molecular Neuroscience

Left: Comparison of the mean scores of CARS and ADI-R questionnaires between the two groups, and also among children with three different genotypes (AA, GG and GA) of rs11102807. Significant difference in CARS scores and all three subscales of ADI-R including social interaction, communication and repetitive behavior between the controls and autistic children (up), and among cases with different genotypes of TRIM33. Right: Comparison of TRIM33 expression at translational level between the two groups using Western blot assay and densitometric analysis of the TRIM33 (or TIF1γ) target protein expression on X-ray films normalized to corresponding β-actin immunoblots in samples from children with autism vs those of the controls. TRIM33 gene expression at translational level is notably higher in normal children compared to those with autism.

Up: Representation of the enrichment map of the schizophrenia gene-pathway network. The circular nodes represent genes, while rectangular nodes represent pathways. Neuroactive ligand-receptor interaction (KEGG ID: hsa04080) is the most central pathway, while mitogen-activated protein kinase-3 (MAP3K) and calcium voltage-gated channel subunit alpha1 C (CACNA1C) genes are the most central genes in the flow of information on the schizophrenia gene-pathway network. Oxytocin receptor gene & impulsivity Back to top

A novel link between the oxytocin receptor gene and impulsivity; 2020, Neuroscience

Up: Representation of the interactive gene network associated with impulsivity. The circular nodes represent the genes associated with impulsivity. The rectangular node represents the found gene (the OXTR gene) having the highest level of interaction with the impulsivity gene set. SLC6A4 is the most central gene in the flow of information in the impulsivity gene network map, having an interaction with the OXTR gene via HTR2A and HTR2B genes which encode serotonin receptors. Down: Linkage disequilibrium (LD) heat map of the seven investigated OXTR SNPs. LD patterns across the whole investigated region are calculated based on different variants of the OXTR gene and three haplotype blocks were obtained according to the D′ values. The ratio of combinations in each block are calculated and shown to the right of each observed haplotype. Obtained combinations of these blocks are shown by bars with two different thicknesses. rs2254298 represents the tag SNP found in our experiments and its most frequent haplotype is TGTTGT. The number on the cells is the LOD score of D′.

Oxytocin & risky decision-making (in gambling) Back to top

Oxytocin moderates risky decision-making during the Iowa Gambling Task: a new insight based on the role of oxytocin receptor gene polymorphisms and interventional cognitive study; 2019, Neuroscience Letters

Left: Linkage disequilibrium (LD) heat map of the 14 found OXTR SNPs. 14 SNPs with MAF > 0.1% in 153 Caucasian individuals (in three groups) were examined for genotypic association with risky behavior in decision-making. LD patterns across the whole investigated region are estimated based on different variants of the OXTR gene and one haplotype block was obtained according to the D′ values. The intersections of the diagonals between pairs of SNPs are marked by colors according to the degree of LD. The color of the cells is set to D′/LOD and the higher values are closer to the red spectrum. GTT is the most frequent haplotype for our select tag SNP. Red rectangle represents the tag SNP found in our experiments. The number on the cell is the LOD score of D′. Right: Comparison of the effect of different treatments and genotypes (CC, CT, and TT) of the selected OXTR SNP on the final decision in IGT to choose a card deck which might be the most rewarding one. Those who received oxytocin mainly chose either deck C or D as their final decision for the most advantageous deck compared to those who received placebo or no treatments, even though this difference was not statistically significant. Those with the TT genotype primarily selected either deck C or D as the most rewarding deck compared to those with the CC and CT genotypes. However, this did not result in a statistically significant difference for deck selection. Adv decks: advantageous or good decks of cards (decks C and D); Disadv decks: disadvantageous or bad decks of cards (decks A and B).
